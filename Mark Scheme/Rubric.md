# Business Proposal: Website and Report Assessment Criteria

| Assessment Criteria | Pass Grade (D) | Typical Excellent Grade (A) | 
 | ----- | ----- | ----- | 
| **Propose a business idea grounded in thorough customer research and the need identified in the market.** | The customer problem is presented but is not sufficiently grounded in the research conducted. | The business idea is based on in-depth customer research and clearly addresses a significant market need. The problem-solution fit is strong and well-supported by the evidence. | 
| **Offer an innovative solution addressing the customer need and demonstrate its feasibility.** | The solution is innovative but is not very clearly connected to the customerâ€™s need. There is not enough evidence that it is feasible. | The solution is innovative, with clear and detailed feasibility demonstrated through well-reasoned arguments and supporting data. | 
| **Design a compelling business case, clearly articulating its financial potential and a sensible revenue model.** | A basic business case is presented, with some financial details and a revenue model. | The business case is compelling and thoroughly articulated, with a robust financial model that clearly demonstrates revenue potential and sustainability and includes a clear unit economics calculation and a multi-year forecast with referenced assumptions. | 
| **Articulate the USP of the proposed solution by presenting and analysing the competitive landscape.** | Competitors are listed but it is not very clear what the USP is. | The competitive landscape is thoroughly analyzed, showing a deep understanding of the market. The USP is clearly articulated and strongly differentiates the venture from competitors. | 
| **Outline an initial idea of a marketing strategy.** | A basic marketing strategy is outlined but lacks detail and alignment with the target audience. | The marketing strategy is detailed and well-aligned with the target audience. The chosen acquisition channels are grounded in market research, incl. primary research. | 
| **Design a fundraising strategy aligned with the type of business you are proposing.** | Fundraising implications of the business design are considered but are not very well aligned with the business or lack supporting evidence. | The fundraising strategy is well thought out, aligned with the business model, and clearly considers different funding sources. It also includes examples of potential investors. | 
| **Connect the business proposal and business model design choices with the market research you have done.** | The key business model considerations are covered, but there are inconsistencies and a lack of supporting evidence. | The business model is clear and coherent. It is closely connected to thorough market research, with clear, logical reasoning linking the two. | 
| **Craft and deliver an engaging narrative that clearly conveys the attractiveness of the proposed opportunity.** | The report is written in a professional manner but the narrative could be significantly improved. | The report has a clear narrative that is engaging, well-crafted, and convincingly conveys the attractiveness of the business opportunity. | 
| **Design a professional report.** | The report is structured and readable but may lack polish and coherence. | The report is highly professional, well-organized, and presents a coherent and persuasive argument. It is visually appealing and free of errors. | 
| **Design an engaging and customer-focused website that effectively communicates the value proposition and encourages user interaction.** | The website is functional with basic content and navigation but lacks elements that engage the customer or encourage interaction. The value proposition is presented but may not be compelling. | The website is highly engaging, with a user-centered design that effectively communicates the value proposition. It includes interactive elements that encourage customer engagement and action. The overall design is visually appealing and aligned with the brand message. |
