# Instruction Manual: The Business Proposal

**Module:** DESE71013 - Innovation Management
**Weighting:** 50% of Final Mark
**Deadline:** 06 March 2026
**Constraint:** Max 5,000 words (Report)

## 1. Project Overview

Must demonstrate that venture is both **customer-desirable** and **investor-viable**.

### The Two Components

1. **Customer-Facing Website:** A professional online presence designed to engage potential users and showcase your value proposition.
2. **Investor/Shareholder Report:** A deep-dive document (max 5,000 words) validating the business opportunity, financials, and strategic roadmap.

## 2. Component A: The Customer-Focused Website

The goal is engagement and conversion.

* **Target Audience:** Potential Customers / Early Adopters.
* **Objective:** Effectively communicate the value proposition and encourage user interaction.
* **Design Requirements:**
    * High-fidelity, professional visual design.
    * Interactive elements (e.g., sign-up forms, demos, or interactive calculators).
    * User-centered navigation.
    * Clear alignment with the brand message.

## 3. Component B: The Investor Report (Structure)

The goal is narrative coherence and analytical depth.

### I. Foundation
* **Executive Summary:** A concise overview of the entire proposal.
* **The Problem:** Clearly define the "pain point" grounded in reality.
* **Research & Validation:** Present primary/secondary research evidence. Link design choices directly to this data.

### II. The Value Proposition
* **The Solution:** Detail innovative response to the identified problem and demonstrate technical/operational feasibility.
* **Competitive Positioning & Market:** Analyze the landscape. Define **USP** and how we strongly differentiate from existing players.

### III. Execution Strategy
* **Go-to-Market Strategy:** Outline marketing strategy, target audience alignment, and chosen acquisition channels.
* **Traction & Roadmap:** What have we achieved so far? What are the key future milestones?

### IV. Commercial Viability
* **The Business Model:** Explain the revenue model, customer journey, and how the venture sustains itself.
* **Financials:** Include unit economics (LTV/CAC), multi-year forecasts, and clearly referenced assumptions.
* **Fundraising Strategy:** Align the "ask" with the business type. Identify potential funding sources and example investors.

### V. The Team
* **The Team:** Showcase the skills and diversity of the founding members.

## 4. Key Learning Objectives

To achieve an **A**,submission must address:

1. **Research-Grounded:** Idea must be based on in-depth customer research.
2. **Innovative & Feasible:** Solution must be novel but supported by feasibility data.
3. **Compelling Business Case:** Robust financial models and clear unit economics.
4. **Articulated USP:** Deep competitive analysis showing clear differentiation.
5. **Marketing Strategy:** Detailed acquisition channels grounded in primary research.
6. **Fundraising Alignment:** Clear logic behind funding sources and investor types.
7. **Coherent Design:** Every business choice must link back to your market research.
8. **Engaging Narrative:** The report must tell a convincing story.
9. **Professionalism:** High-quality organization, visual appeal, and error-free writing.
10. **Interactive Website:** User-centered design that drives engagement and action.
